Set theory

2020-11-06
  1. Suppose \(Y\subset X\) and \(E\) is open relative to \(Y\), then, to each \(p\in E\), there is a positive number \(r_p\) and the condition \(q\in Y\), \(d(p,q)<r_p\) imply that \(q\in E\). Let \(V_p\) be the set of all \(q\in X\) such that \(d(p,q)<r_p\) and define \[G=\underset{p\in E}{\cup} V_p\] Then \(G\) is an open subset of \(X\), since \(p\in V_p\) for all \(p\in E\), it is clear that \(E\subset G\cap Y\). By our choice of \(V_p\), we have \(V_p\cap Y\subset E\) for every \(p\in E\), so that \(G\cap Y\subset E\). Then \[E=G\cap Y\] Conversely, if \(G\) is open in \(X\) and \(E=G\cap Y\), every \(p\in E\) has a neighborhood \(V_p\in G\), then \(V_p\cap Y\subset E\), so that \(E\) is open relative to \(Y\). Then \(E=G\cap Y\). Then, suppose \(Y\subset X\), a subset \(E\) of \(Y\) is open relative to \(Y\) if and only if \(E=Y\cap G\) for some open subset \(G\) of \(X\).
