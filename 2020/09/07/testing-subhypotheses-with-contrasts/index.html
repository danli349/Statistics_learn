Testing Subhypotheses with Contrasts

2020-09-07

A linear combination of the true means of \(k\) factor levels \(\mu_1,\mu_2,\cdots,\mu_k\) of the randomized-one-factor-design \(C=\displaystyle\sum_{j=1}^{k}c_j\mu_j\) is said to be a contrast if the sum of its coefficients \(\displaystyle\sum_{j=1}^{k}c_j=0\). Because \(\overline Y_{.j}\) is always an unbiased estimator for \(\mu_j\), we can use it to estimate C \(\hat C=\displaystyle\sum_{j=1}^{k}c_j\overline Y_{.j}\).
Then, \(E(\hat C=)=\displaystyle\sum_{j=1}^{k}c_jE(\overline Y_{.j})=C=\displaystyle\sum_{j=1}^{k}c_j\mu_j=C\)
