The Multivariate Normal Density

2020-09-11

The univariate normal pdf is: \[f_X(x)=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}\sigma}e^{-\frac{1}{2}(\frac{x-\mu}{\sigma})^2}, \quad -\infty<x<+\infty\] The term \((\frac{x-\mu}{\sigma})^2=(x-\mu)(\sigma^2)^{-1}(x-\mu)\) measures the square of the univariate distance from \(x\) to \(\mu\) in standard deviation units. This can be generalized to a \(p\times 1\) vector \(\mathbf x\) of observations on several variables as \((\mathbf x-\boldsymbol \mu)(\mathbf \Sigma)^{-1}(\mathbf x-\boldsymbol \mu)\), which is the square of the multivariate generalized distance from \(\mathbf x\) to \(\boldsymbol \mu\), the \(p\times p\) matrix \(\mathbf \Sigma\) is the varianceâ€“covariance matrix of \(\mathbf X\). Because the volume under the surface of the multivariate density function unity for any \(p\) is \((2\pi)^{-p/2}|\boldsymbol\Sigma|^{-1/2}\), then a \(p\)-dimensional normal density for the random vector \(\mathbf X^T=[X_1,X_2,\cdots,X_p]\) has the form \[ f_X(\mathbf x)=\frac{1}{(2\pi)^{p/2}|\mathbf\Sigma|^{1/2}}e^{-\frac{1}{2}(\mathbf x-\boldsymbol \mu)^T(\mathbf\Sigma)^{-1}(\mathbf x-\boldsymbol \mu)}, \quad -\infty<x_i<+\infty, \quad i=1,2,\cdots,p \], which can be denoted by \(N_p(\boldsymbol \mu, \mathbf\Sigma)\)
